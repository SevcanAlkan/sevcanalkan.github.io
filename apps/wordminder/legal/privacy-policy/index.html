Information collected

Depending on the Wordminder feature you use, information may include Sign in with Apple account identifiers, collection and word data you create, practice statistics, preferences, support messages, diagnostics, crash logs, usage analytics, and content needed to deliver the service.

If a specific release of Wordminder does not require a certain service or provider, the related data may not be processed for that release.

How information is used

Information may be used to:

  • operate, maintain, and improve Wordminder and related support services;
  • sync collections, words, and practice information across your devices;
  • respond to support requests and communicate about updates;
  • monitor reliability, performance, abuse, and security; and
  • comply with legal obligations and enforce service rules.

Lawful bases for processing

Depending on the Wordminder feature and how you use it, personal data may be processed because it is necessary to provide the service you requested, to comply with legal obligations, for legitimate interests such as security, support, product reliability, and abuse prevention, or on the basis of your consent where consent is required.

Where processing depends on consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time through app settings, device permissions, or by contacting support, but this will not affect processing already carried out before withdrawal.

Third-party services

A specific release of Wordminder may rely on services such as Apple APIs, Sign in with Apple, CloudKit, notification services, analytics tools, crash reporting platforms, or payment processors only where those services are actually included in that release or feature. Those providers may process limited data on behalf of Wordminder or under their own privacy terms.

Data sharing

Personal information is not sold. Information may be shared only when needed to run the service, sync data, process payments, comply with the law, protect users, or support legitimate business operations tied to Wordminder.

International transfers

If Wordminder uses providers located outside your country or the European Economic Area, personal data may be transferred internationally. Where required, appropriate safeguards will be used for those transfers.

Retention and security

Information is kept only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this policy, including support, security, accounting, and legal compliance. Reasonable technical and organizational safeguards are used, but no system can guarantee absolute security.

Your rights and choices

You may be able to control notifications, preferences, analytics sharing, account data, or app language directly inside Wordminder, in device settings, or by contacting support. Available choices depend on the specific feature and architecture.

Depending on applicable law, you may also have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, or object to the processing of your personal data, and where applicable to data portability.

If you are in the European Union, you may lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority. In Bulgaria, the supervisory authority is the Commission for Personal Data Protection (CPDP): cpdp.bg.

Required data and automated decisions

Providing personal data is generally voluntary, but some information may be necessary to deliver a requested feature, maintain an account, sync data, process a purchase, respond to support, or comply with law. If required information is not provided, the relevant feature may not be available.

Personal data is not used for solely automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you unless a specific Wordminder feature clearly states otherwise.

Children's privacy

Wordminder is not intentionally designed to collect personal information from children unless explicitly stated in the product itself. If you believe a child has provided personal information, contact support so the matter can be reviewed and addressed.

Policy changes

This policy may be updated from time to time as Wordminder evolves, providers change, or legal obligations require it. The updated version becomes effective when published on this page.
